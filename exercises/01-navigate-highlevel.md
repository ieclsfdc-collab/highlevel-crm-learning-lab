# Exercise 01: Navigate HighLevel

## Goal

Learn where the main HighLevel areas are and practice using GHL terminology instead of Salesforce terminology.

## Estimated Time

45 to 60 minutes

## Starting Point

Start inside a test sub-account/location in HighLevel.

Do not use real client data.

## What You Will Create

Nothing yet. This exercise is for navigation and terminology.

## Milestone 1: Identify Your Workspace

### Steps

1. Log in to HighLevel.
2. Confirm whether you are viewing an Agency account or a Sub-account/Location.
3. Write down the name of the workspace you are inside.
4. Do not proceed inside a real client workspace unless you are only observing.

### Expected Result

You know whether you are at agency level or inside a specific sub-account/location.

### Milestone Summary

In this milestone, you learned that:

- HighLevel can have an agency-level view and sub-account/location views.
- Most CRM learning should happen inside a safe test sub-account/location.
- The correct GHL term is Sub-account or Location, not Salesforce Org.

### Remember This in GHL Terms

A Sub-account or Location is the workspace where a business manages Contacts, Opportunities, Workflows, Calendars, and Conversations.

### Quick Self-Check

1. Am I in an Agency account or a Sub-account/Location?
2. What is the name of the workspace?
3. Why should I avoid real client data?
4. What Salesforce term should I avoid using here?
5. What could the Orchestrator inspect at this level?

## Milestone 2: Find the Main CRM Areas

### Steps

1. Use the left-side menu.
2. Find Contacts.
3. Find Opportunities.
4. Find Conversations.
5. Find Calendars.
6. Find Automation or Workflows.
7. Find Sites, Forms, Funnels, or Surveys.
8. Find Settings.
9. Write each menu name exactly as it appears in your account.

### Expected Result

You have a short map of the HighLevel menu labels in your account.

### Milestone Summary

In this milestone, you learned that:

- Contacts are central person records.
- Opportunities are tracked inside Pipelines.
- Workflows usually live under Automation.
- Forms, Funnels, Calendars, and Conversations support the lead journey.

### Remember This in GHL Terms

A Contact can enter through a Form or Funnel. A Workflow can act on the Contact. An Opportunity can track the Contact inside a Pipeline.

### Quick Self-Check

1. Where do I find Contacts?
2. Where do I find Opportunities?
3. Where do I find Workflows?
4. Where do I find Forms or Funnels?
5. Which menu areas could matter during troubleshooting?

3. Where are Workflows found?
4. Where are Opportunities found?
5. What GHL terms replaced Salesforce Org, Lead, Flow, and Activity Timeline?
