# Exercise 04: Create Form and Submit Test Lead

## Goal

Create a basic Form and submit a safe test lead so MJ understands how Contacts can enter HighLevel.

## Estimated Time

60 minutes

## Starting Point

Start inside a test sub-account/location.

Do not use real client data.

## What You Will Create

| Item Type | Exact Name to Use |
|---|---|
| Form | MJ Test Lead Form |
| Test submission | Test Lead - Form Submit |

## Milestone 1: Create a Form

### Steps

1. Go to Sites, Forms, or the area where Forms are managed.
2. Create a new Form named `MJ Test Lead Form`.
3. Add fields for:
   - First Name
   - Last Name
   - Email
   - Service Interest, if available
4. Save the Form.

### Expected Result

You can see a Form named `MJ Test Lead Form`.

### Milestone Summary

In this milestone, you learned that:

- A Form is a lead capture tool in HighLevel.
- Form fields collect Contact information.
- Form submissions can create or update Contacts.

### Remember This in GHL Terms

A Form captures lead information. A Contact can enter HighLevel through a Form.

### Quick Self-Check

1. What Form did I create?
2. Which fields did I add?
3. Where can I find the Form again?
4. How does a Form connect to a Contact?
5. What could the Orchestrator inspect on Forms?

## Milestone 2: Submit a Test Lead

### Steps

1. Preview or open the Form.
2. Submit safe test information:
   - First Name: `Test Lead`
   - Last Name: `Form Submit`
   - Email: use a non-real placeholder email
   - Service Interest: `CRM Audit`
3. Submit the Form.
4. Go to Contacts.
5. Search for the test Contact you submitted.

### Expected Result

A Contact exists for the Form submission.

### Milestone Summary

In this milestone, you learned that:

- A Form submission can create or update a Contact.
- The Contact is where the captured lead information appears.
- The lead journey can start from a Form.

### Remember This in GHL Terms

A Contact entered HighLevel through a Form. The Form captured the Contact information.

### Quick Self-Check

1. What test Contact was created?
2. Where did the Contact come from?
3. What information was captured?
4. How could this trigger a Workflow later?
5. What could fail in a real Form submission?
